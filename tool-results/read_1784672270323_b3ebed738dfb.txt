    47вҶ’## 1. Overview
    48вҶ’
    49вҶ’### 1.1 Purpose
    50вҶ’
    51вҶ’Location Intelligence is not a feature bolted onto GGH вҖ” it is a foundational capability woven into every layer of the platform. In a wholesale grocery delivery business, **where things are** determines **what can be sold, when it can arrive, who delivers it, and how much it costs**.
    52вҶ’
    53вҶ’Without location intelligence, GGH cannot:
    54вҶ’
    55вҶ’- Tell a customer when their 25 kg rice sack will arrive
    56вҶ’- Assign the right driver from the right warehouse
    57вҶ’- Optimize routes for a fleet of 50+ delivery vehicles across Cairo
    58вҶ’- Prevent warehouse stockouts by rebalancing inventory across regions
    59вҶ’- Provide the "Uber-like" tracking experience Egyptian consumers now expect
    60вҶ’
    61вҶ’### 1.2 Maps as a Core Primitive
    62вҶ’
    63вҶ’Maps are not just a pin on a screen. In GGH, maps become the **visual and computational substrate** for:
    64вҶ’
    65вҶ’| Domain | How Maps Are Core |
    66вҶ’|--------|-------------------|
    67вҶ’| **ERP** | Warehouse locations, inventory distribution, regional stock visibility |
    68вҶ’| **Warehouse Management** | Coverage radius, zone assignment, capacity heatmaps, proximity-based dispatch |
    69вҶ’| **Deliveries** | Route computation, ETA calculation, live tracking, proof-of-location delivery confirmation |
    70вҶ’| **Customer Experience** | Pin-drop addressing, saved locations, live driver tracking, delivery notifications |
    71вҶ’| **Fleet Monitoring** | Vehicle positions, speed, heading, idle detection, geofence violations |
    72вҶ’
    73вҶ’### 1.3 Guiding Principles
    74вҶ’
    75вҶ’| Principle | Description |
    76вҶ’|-----------|-------------|
    77вҶ’| **Location-First Thinking** | Every entity that exists in the physical world (warehouse, driver, customer, order) has coordinates |
    78вҶ’| **ERP as Source of Truth** | ERPNext owns warehouse locations, driver records, fleet data; GGH reads and enhances |
    79вҶ’| **Eventually Consistent** | GPS positions may lag 2вҖ“5 seconds; warehouse inventory syncs every 5 minutes |
    80вҶ’| **Never Block the Customer** | If GPS fails, fall back to zone-based estimates; if maps fail, show text-based status |
    81вҶ’| **Elder-Friendly** | Om Ibrahim (62) must understand tracking with zero technical knowledge |
    82вҶ’| **Egypt-First** | Optimize for Egyptian addresses (landmarks > street numbers), Arabic-first UI, local mapping data |
    83вҶ’| **Offline-Resilient** | Drivers in areas with poor connectivity must still function; queue and sync later |

   101вҶ’## 2. Business Goals
   102вҶ’
   103вҶ’### 2.1 Why GPS Matters for GGH
   104вҶ’
   105вҶ’GGH operates in Egyptian cities where:
   106вҶ’
   107вҶ’- Addresses are landmark-based ("behind the mosque, next to the pharmacy")
   108вҶ’- Traffic is unpredictable вҖ” a 3 km delivery can take 10 minutes or 60 minutes
   109вҶ’- Customers expect to **see** their delivery approaching, not just receive a phone call
   110вҶ’- Warehouse density determines delivery speed and cost
   111вҶ’- Driver efficiency directly impacts profitability
   112вҶ’
   113вҶ’### 2.2 Business Objectives
   114вҶ’
   115вҶ’| # | Objective | Metric | Target |
   116вҶ’|---|-----------|--------|--------|
   117вҶ’| BG-1 | Live delivery tracking | % of active orders trackable | вүҘ 99% |
   118вҶ’| BG-2 | Warehouse visibility | % of inventory with warehouse coords | 100% |
   119вҶ’| BG-3 | Driver monitoring | % of drivers with live GPS | вүҘ 95% |
   120вҶ’| BG-4 | Route optimization | Avg. delivery time reduction | вүҘ 20% |
   121вҶ’| BG-5 | Delivery ETA accuracy | ETA within Вұ10 min of actual | вүҘ 85% |
   122вҶ’| BG-6 | Proof of delivery | % of deliveries with GPS+photo confirmation | вүҘ 98% |
   123вҶ’| BG-7 | Logistics analytics | Fleet utilization rate | вүҘ 75% |
   124вҶ’| BG-8 | Customer satisfaction | "Can I track my order?" support tickets | вҶ“ 80% |
   125вҶ’| BG-9 | Cost reduction | Cost per delivery (EGP) | вҶ“ 15% |
   126вҶ’| BG-10 | Delivery zone coverage | % of Greater Cairo addressable | вүҘ 90% |

   526вҶ’### 5.6 Pin Drop Workflow
   527вҶ’
   528вҶ’1. Customer opens map centered on their current GPS position
   529вҶ’2. Map shows a draggable pin at detected location
   530вҶ’3. Reverse geocoding fills address fields automatically
   531вҶ’4. Customer can drag pin to adjust exact position
   532вҶ’5. Customer fills apartment, floor, building, landmark
   533вҶ’6. System validates: accuracy, coverage zone, nearest warehouse
   534вҶ’7. Customer saves location with label (home/work/other)

   846вҶ’## 9. Live Delivery Tracking
   847вҶ’
   848вҶ’### 9.1 Tracking Experience Philosophy
   849вҶ’
   850вҶ’The GGH tracking experience must match the expectations set by **Uber, Talabat, and DoorDash**. Egyptian consumers have been trained by ride-hailing and food delivery apps to expect:
   851вҶ’
   852вҶ’1. **See the driver on a map in real time**
   853вҶ’2. **Know the exact ETA**
   854вҶ’3. **See the route the driver will take**
   855вҶ’4. **Contact the driver with one tap**
   856вҶ’5. **Know when the driver has arrived**
   857вҶ’
   858вҶ’For **Om Ibrahim** (62, low tech comfort), the tracking must be **simpler than Uber**:
   859вҶ’- Large text, large icons
   860вҶ’- One status at a time (not multiple simultaneous updates)
   861вҶ’- Clear Arabic: "Ш§Щ„ШіШ§ШҰЩӮ ЩҒЩҠ Ш§Щ„Ш·ШұЩҠЩӮ" (Driver is on the way)
   862вҶ’- One button to call the driver
